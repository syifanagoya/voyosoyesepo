Early Life & Biography

Jakob Dylan was born on December 9, 1969, in New York to parents Sara and Bob Dylan. His father, Bob Dylan, is one of the greatest musicians to have ever lived. Jakob grew up to continue his father’s legacy in creating extraordinary music. He was the youngest among the five siblings. In the early years of his life, Jakob resided in Greenwich Village. When he was around three years old, the family shifted to the Los Angeles area.

While growing up, Jakob showed interest in English rock records his elder brother maintained. Few of those included the Clash, the Jam, and the Buzzcocks. Listening to the Clash, Jakob got inspired enough to start exploring the world of music himself. While in high school, Dylan became a part of various bands as a guitarist.

He was featured on one of his friend’s band’s eponymous album in 1987 titled “Trash Matinee” as a guitarist. Jakob used to be a part of the band known as the Bootheels, along with his future Wallflower bad member Tobi Miller. It was at this time, Jakob had started writing his songs and compositions.

He slowly moved from being a guitarist to a singer and songwriter. After Jakob graduated from high school, he decided to shift to New York City to study art at the Parsons School of Design. Jakob dropped out in the first semester itself and moved back to LA in pursuit of his dreams and passion for creating music.

Career

Jakob formed the Wallflowers along with guitarist Tobi Miller, keyboard player Rami Jaffee, Barrie Maguire on bass, and Peter Yanowitz on the drums. The band signed a contract with the Virgin and released their first self titled debut album in 1992. The record didn’t mark good sales records, and Virgin revoked the contract.

The band later got resurrected with members like Michael Ward on guitar, Greg Richling on bass, and Mario Claire on drums formed the new Wallflowers. The band then signed with Interscope Records and released an album along with producer T Bone Burnett. In 1996, they released the infamous song “Bringing Down the Horses” and “6th Avenue Heartache”.

The band also released the hit single “One Headlight” next year. It secured the spot on the Top Ten Hitlist, which helped in establishing the Wallflower as a legitimate commercial band. The band took a break then and later on made it was the return on October 2000. Their efforts to reach the top charts one more time were ignored by the mainstream radio.

The band later came to an end with their contract with Interscope Records after releasing “Rebels, Sweetheart.” Dylan then signed a deal with Columbia Records, which also served as his father’s label partners for a long period in 2006. Dylan released his solo album “Women + Country” in 2010, which quickly became his all-time greatest hit.
